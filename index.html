About

I am a PhD student at the ASU Department of Physics. My primary research focuses on the development of highly sensitive Rb-Xe-He comagnetometers which utilize SQUIDS (Superconducting Quantum Interference Devices) to achieve precision measurements of fundamental physics such as dark matter and axion searches.

Theory

Alongside instrumentation, I maintain active research in theoretical cosmology, specifically calculating Next-to-Leading-Log (NLL) corrections to stochastic inflation in de-Sitter space.

Publications & Talks

  • Advances in nuclear spin coherent control, and in SQUID design, for a new measurement of the xenon-129 EDM.
